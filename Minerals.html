Mathematical Minerals

Mathematical Minerals (Work in progess) is a research project. It began with a small box of stones that I rediscovered, which once belonged to my mother. When I started studying the stones, I discovered that they contain incredibly geometric structures.

In my research, I explore ways of approaching this geometry within an artisanal practice. I work with techniques such as lino printing, etching, sawing, and soldering. By reducing forms to 2D, I investigate what happens when crystal structures are translated into another material and surface. This project is an ongoing exploration of geometry, perfection, and craft. It is a process that continues to evolve.
